?

Cryptocurrency consensus follows the same principles as consensus in real-world applications, except with computers. These computers will come to an agreement on whether a transaction is valid or not, based on a consensus threshold. If the threshold is more than half or 51%, the transaction will be valid and vice versa. The primary difference is that Cryptocurrency consensus mechanisms have built-in financial incentives to make sure valid transactions are confirmed.

Because cryptocurrency blockchains are based on approving millions and sometimes billions of transactions, stakes are much higher. This requires someone to connect additional computers to a cryptocurrency blockchain to “manipulate” these transactions to their own benefit. This is called consensus manipulation.

Therefore, 2 cryptocurrency mining methods: Proof-of-Work (PoW) and Proof-of-Stake (PoS) are introduced.

What is Proof-of- Work? (PoW)

Proof-of-Work was invented in 1993 to combat email spam and to ensure the security of a distributed digital payments network.
Any computer who wants to process transactions on a PoW cryptocurrency blockchain like Bitcoin(BTC), needs to make a correct guess of an extremely random number to have the right to make transactions. This process costs both time and energy to do. The likelihood of a computer to solve this problem is based on the hashing / computing power. Higher the power, higher the likelihood of guessing the number and getting the opportunity to produce a bitcoin block containing transactions.
As an incentive to join PoW cryptocurrency blockchain, you earn fees from the transactions of each block, plus a block reward in that cryptocurrency's native coin, in this case BTC.
The computer that guesses the random number correctly first will get the right to make transactions and a new random number will be generated. The same computer will be able to do consecutive transactions if it is capable of guess the number first again.
To prevent supercomputers from guessing the random number before anyone else, cryptocurrency coins like Bitcoin will adjust the difficulty of these numbers based on how much computing power is connected to the cryptocurrency blockchain.
If someone wanted to attack a PoW cryptocurrency, they would need more than just powerful computers and lots of energy. The computers need to be continuously producing faulty blocks in a short span of time, ensuring their faulty transactions go through.
As soon as a conflicting set of transactions on the blockchain, the blockchain will temporarily fork. The other computers will continue building on the longest chain. This longest chain will be hard to maintain for an entity that's manipulating the network, thus making a PoW cryptocurrency with a long history more secure.

Pros
  • Higher security level
  • Lets the miners earn crypto rewards
  • Provides a decentralised method for verifying transactions
Cons
  • High consumption of energy.
  • Expensive fees with low transaction speed.
  • Expensive equipment required for mining

What is Proof-of-Stake? (PoS)

This method works with a cryptocurrency coin that is “staked” I.E locked on the blockchain to earn the right to process transactions.
The length of time of a cryptocurrency must be staked to process transactions as can the minimum amount of coins or tokens a computer must lock up as a “stake”. The more cryptocurrency you stake, the more likely you are to process transactions and produce a block.
PoS consensus mechanisms employ some degree of randomness to determine which computer gets to produce a block containing transactions.
Aka giving more dice to throw depending on staking amount. Higher probability to roll higher than everyone else, but not guaranteed to be rolling higher than everyone else every time. PoS also uses “slashing”. When a computer breaks the rules in the blockchain, some of their cryptocurrency staked will be destroyed.
Not everyone is able to run their computers 24/7 or don’t have the minimum stake required to join a network. Users can lend their cryptocurrency to another computer to earn a cut of their rewards.
This staking process makes it easy to create an unchained government system that allows stakers to vote on how the cryptocurrency is changed. E.g: minimum amount of crypto stake can be lowered for more participation, staking lock-up period can be extended to ensure the network is secure as more computers join due to the lowered staking.
PoS also makes transactions more efficient. Putting down money and throwing dice is faster than solving increasing complex crossword puzzles.

Pros
  • Does not require expensive equipment for participation.
  • Fast and inexpensive transaction speed.
  • Energy-efficient.
Cons
  • Lacks in terms of security when compared to proof-of-work.
  • Validators with large holdings can influence the verification of transactions
  • Few of the PoS cryptos require locking up staked coins for a certain period of time.
